Sprint context (sprint number, team, notable events, or focus areas): $ARGUMENTS

Facilitate a structured, productive retrospective that surfaces actionable improvements. Keep the tone constructive and psychologically safe — focus on systems and processes, not individuals.

---

## Retrospective Setup

**Format:** Choose the most appropriate format based on team needs:

- **Standard (Start / Stop / Continue)**: Good for general retrospectives
- **4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For)**: Good for exploring feelings and learning
- **Sailboat (Wind / Anchors / Rocks / Sun)**: Good for identifying risks and opportunities
- **Mad / Sad / Glad**: Good for teams going through change or high-stress sprints
- **Timeline**: Good for sprints with significant incidents or complex delivery

Based on the context provided, suggest the most suitable format and run it.

---

## Part 1: Set the Stage

**Ground rules** (remind the team):
- Prime directive: "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, resources available, and the situation at hand."
- Focus on process and systems, not individuals
- One conversation at a time
- Everything discussed stays in the room (unless we decide to share specific action items)

**Check-in activity** (pick one):
- Weather check: each person describes their sprint experience as weather (sunny / cloudy / stormy / foggy)
- One word: describe the sprint in one word, round robin
- Quick win: share one thing that went well this sprint

## Part 4: Action Items

The most critical output. Action items should be:
- **Specific**: "We will do X" not "We should improve Y"
- **Owned**: One named person responsible
- **Time-bound**: Committed by a specific date or sprint
- **Small**: Achievable within one sprint; don't over-commit
- **Measurable**: How will we know it worked?

## Facilitation Tips

- **Silence is data**: If the team is quiet on "what didn't go well," the psychological safety may not be there. Try anonymous input tools (Miro, Mentimeter, sticky notes).
- **Equal voice**: If one or two people dominate, use dot voting or round-robin to ensure everyone contributes.
- **Time box**: Keep data gathering to 20 minutes, analysis to 15 minutes, action items to 10 minutes.
- **Follow through**: The biggest killer of retro trust is action items that are never checked on. Open the next retro by reviewing this sprint's commitments.
